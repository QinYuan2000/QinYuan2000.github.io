Hello!

I recently completed my Master's degree in Electrical Engineering and Information Technology at ETH Zurich, where I worked with Prof. Lana Josipović. Previously, I obtained my B.Eng. in Electronic Science and Technology from Southeast University, where I was advised by Prof. Peng Cao.

My research interests lie in high-level synthesis, dynamically scheduled dataflow circuits, and hardware optimization. I am especially interested in the algorithmic and mathematical aspects of circuit generation, including token delivery, control-flow analysis, buffer placement, throughput modeling, and optimization problems formulated through integer programming. My recent work focuses on Fast Token Delivery in elastic circuits, where I developed a robust suppression algorithm and implemented it in Dynamatic, an open-source HLS compiler.

Selected Projects

  • A Systematic Approach to Robust Fast Token Delivery
    Master's Thesis, ETH Zurich, 2026

    Developed a complete Fast Token Delivery procedure for dynamically scheduled dataflow circuits. The work formalizes GSA conversion, regeneration, and suppression, and introduces a suppression algorithm that resolves internal condition-token deliveries during construction. Implemented the algorithm in Dynamatic and integrated it into the compiler flow.

  • Refactoring and Extending MILP-Based Buffer Placement
    ETH Zurich, 2024 - 2025

    Redesigned Dynamatic's buffer placement infrastructure to support diverse buffer types and algorithmic extensions. Implemented a new optimization algorithm and resolved critical modeling issues to improve circuit performance and ensure correct modeling.

  • Cost-Aware Buffer Placement for Dataflow Circuits
    ETH Zurich, 2024

    Proposed a MILP model that selects low-area buffers while preserving throughput. The project focused on area-aware buffer selection, timing constraints, and performance preservation in dynamically scheduled dataflow circuits.

  • Statistical Cell Delay Modeling under Input Slew Variation
    Southeast University, 2023

    Built a statistical delay model for sub-threshold inverter chains under correlated Vth and input slew variations. Used piecewise-log input approximation to derive closed-form delay expressions and validated the model through Monte Carlo simulation on a 28 nm PDK.

  • Racing Track Calibration via Cone Tracking
    UC Irvine, Remote, 2022

    Tracked roadside cones from onboard video and aligned them to vehicle coordinates. Optimized GPS trajectory using cone-to-vehicle geometry via Gurobi.

  • Flip-Flop Timing Optimization for NTV Design
    Southeast University, 2021 - 2022

    Built an ANN-based timing model capturing interdependent setup/hold and clk-to-q delays in NTV flip-flops. Integrated it into STA with a GA-based optimizer for flip-flop timing optimization.
