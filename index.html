Hello!

I am a second-year Master's student at the Department of Information Technology and Electrical Engineering, ETH Zurich, advised by Prof. Lana Josipović. Previously, I obtained B.Eng. in Electronic Science and Technology from Southeast University, where I was advised by Prof. Peng Cao.

I am broadly interested in the algorithmic and mathematical aspects of High-Level Synthesis and hardware optimization. My current research explores problems related to buffer placement, throughput modeling, and dataflow circuit transformations, from the perspectives of integer programming, graph algorithms, and system-level design automation. I contribute to Dynamatic, an open-source HLS compiler, with upstream changes involving cost-aware modeling and structural optimization.

My hobbies include classical music, particularly symphonies and operas, playing trombone and clarinet, as well as football, snooker, and karting.

Selected Projects

  • Refactoring & Extending MILP-Based Buffer Placement
    ETH Zurich, 2024 - 2025

    Redesigned Dynamatic's buffer placement infrastructure with modular MILP formulation, cost-aware objective, and throughput-preserving constraints. Achieved 100× optimization speedup via two-stage MIQCP solving.

  • Cost-Aware Buffer Placement for Dataflow Circuits
    ETH Zurich, 2024

    Proposed a latency-bound MILP model to select low-area buffers while preserving throughput. Achieved 4.8-25.5% LUT reduction on PolyBench without performance loss.

  • Statistical Cell Delay Modeling under Input Slew Variation
    Southeast University, 2023

    Derived unified delay model via piecewise-log input slews; validated on 28nm PDK with 4% error in HSPICE Monte Carlo.

  • Racing Track Calibration via Cone Tracking
    UC Irvine (Remote), 2022

    Detected cones from onboard videos and used vehicle dynamics to calibrate GPS track via Gurobi-based optimization.

  • Flip-Flop Timing Optimization for NTV Design
    Southeast University, 2021 - 2022

    Developed ANN-based interdependent FF timing model and integrated it into STA flow; improved performance by 1.7-6.3% on ISCAS'89 benchmarks.
